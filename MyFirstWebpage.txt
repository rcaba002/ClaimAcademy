Front-End Web Development Cohort

Introduction

This class will teach you the fundamentals of how the web works and gain a working knowledge of the three foundational languages that power each and every website: HTML, CSS and JavaScript. This class will provide a guided, efficient path for you to learn to build responsive websites. 

By the end of the class you’ll have built a diverse portfolio of projects to show friends, family, and employers.

Featured Student


To be successful in this class you should follow these general guidelines:

First, you must be self-driven and motivated to learn. Participation in this class requires consistently meeting the deadlines set for this cohort and devoting at least 6 hours per week to your work.

Second, you can communicate fluently and professionally in written and spoken English.

Third, you are willing to contribute to the success of the program, including collaborating with fellow students and giving us feedback on how we can improve.


Conclusion

Never stop learning. Web development changes quickly, arguably faster than almost any other development genre. Practice! Practice! Practice! It's one of the best ways to really challenge yourself, and keep growing. Be open to change. You might think you know what area of web development you want to go into, but you’ll definitely discover new interests along the way. Come to web projects open minded, willing to tackle any challenge. You’ll form your strengths and specializations later in your career.
